Biologic inhibitors target the C-Met receptor, while small molecules inhibit the tyrosine kinase activity of C-Met. Antagonist antibodies block the binding of HGF to C-Met, while Kringle variant antagonists disrupt HGF dimerization. These inhibitors are used in the treatment of various cancers and other diseases associated with dysregulated C-Met signaling.</p></p>
