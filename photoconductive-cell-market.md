<p><p>Photoconductive cells are light-sensitive devices that change their electrical conductivity when exposed to light. There are two main types on the market: Cadmium Sulphide and Cadmium Selenide cells. Cadmium Sulphide cells are commonly used in applications where a low-cost solution is needed, such as in light-sensitive alarms and automatic lighting controls. On the other hand, Cadmium Selenide cells are more expensive but offer higher sensitivity and faster response times, making them suitable for high-precision applications like photometers and light meters.</p></p>
