<p><p>Radar liquid level transmitters operate using different technologies, including FMCW radar, guided wave, CW radar, and pulse radar. FMCW radar uses frequency-modulated continuous waves for more precise measurements. Guided wave transmitters use a probe to send and receive signals, suitable for challenging environments. CW radar emits continuous waves to measure levels accurately. Pulse radar transmitters release short pulses of radar waves for versatile applications. Each type of radar liquid level transmitter has its strengths in terms of accuracy, reliability, and performance in various settings.</p></p>
